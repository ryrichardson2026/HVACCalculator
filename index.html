Repair or Replace — FAQ
Replacement costs vary significantly based on your system type (gas furnace, electric furnace, heat pump) and your home size. The range across those variables is wide enough that a ballpark number without your specifics won't be useful. Use our free calculator — answer 5 quick questions and get an instant estimate specific to your home and city.
A furnace burns fuel (gas or electric resistance) to generate heat. A heat pump moves heat from outside air into your home — it's more efficient in mild climates like the Pacific Northwest and also cools your home in summer, making it a two-in-one system. Gas furnaces heat faster in very cold snaps; heat pumps cost less to run day-to-day and qualify for Snohomish PUD rebates. Which is right for your home depends on your current system, age, and situation. Run the calculator and we'll tell you which direction makes sense.
Simple maintenance like filter replacement is fine to do yourself. Anything involving gas lines, electrical connections, or the heat exchanger requires a licensed technician — both for safety and to avoid voiding your warranty. Washington State also requires permits for furnace replacement. Not sure whether your issue is a quick fix or something bigger? Use the calculator to get a repair vs replace recommendation first.
System age is the biggest factor. If your furnace is over 15 years old, replacement usually makes more long-term financial sense — especially when you factor in PUD rebates on heat pumps. A useful rule of thumb: if repair cost exceeds 30% of replacement value, replace. The right answer depends on your specific system, problem, and home size. Run our 5-question calculator for a personalized recommendation.
Yes. Snohomish County PUD offers rebates for qualifying heat pump installations, and federal tax credits may also apply depending on system type and income. Exact amounts depend on the system — your installer can confirm eligibility during the free estimate. Use the calculator to see if a heat pump is the right recommendation for your home.
Most replacements in Mill Creek are completed in one day — typically 4–8 hours. Jobs requiring new ductwork may take 2–3 days. Your technician will give you a realistic timeframe after the free estimate. Not sure if replacement is even the right call? Start with the calculator to find out.
