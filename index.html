Common Questions
Answers specific to Mill Creek & Snohomish County
AC installation in Mill Creek typically runs $4,500–$8,500 for a system replacement and $5,500–$10,500 for a new install in a home with existing ductwork. Homes without existing ducts should consider a ductless mini split, which avoids the $2,500–$5,500 ductwork cost entirely.
Single-zone mini split installation in Snohomish County runs $3,500–$5,500. Two-zone systems cost $6,500–$9,500. Three-zone systems run $10,500–$15,000. Whole-home multi-zone systems start at $15,000 and can exceed $22,000 depending on the number of zones and brand.
Many older homes in Mill Creek and Snohomish County were built without ductwork — especially split-level and rambler-style homes common in this area. If no ducts exist, a ductless mini split is usually the most cost-effective path. If ducts exist but need work, budget an additional $2,500–$5,500.
Central AC only cools your home. A heat pump both cools in summer and heats in winter — effectively replacing your furnace and AC in one system. In the Pacific Northwest climate, heat pumps are increasingly popular for their year-round efficiency and eligibility for utility rebates from Snohomish PUD and PSE.
Yes. Snohomish County PUD and Puget Sound Energy both offer rebates on qualifying heat pump installations — typically $500–$1,500 depending on the system and your utility provider. Federal tax credits may also apply. Ask your HVAC pro about current programs before purchasing.
For a 1,500–2,500 sqft home in Snohomish County, expect $5,500–$8,500 for a central AC replacement, or $7,000–$10,500 for a new install where ductwork already exists. Add $2,500–$5,500 if new ductwork is needed.
Most standard AC installations in Mill Creek are completed in one day — typically 4–8 hours. Jobs requiring new ductwork may take 2–3 days. Your technician will give you a realistic timeframe after the free in-home estimate.
Yes. Most AC and heat pump installations in Washington State require a permit, which your licensed contractor pulls on your behalf. Permit fees in Snohomish County typically run $150–$400 depending on your city. This ensures the work is inspected and up to code.
AC sizing depends on your home's square footage, ceiling height, insulation, and sun exposure. A licensed technician will perform a Manual J load calculation during the estimate to recommend the correct size. Both oversized and undersized systems cause efficiency and comfort problems.
Most HVAC professionals in Snohomish County work with major brands including Carrier, Trane, Lennox, Rheem, and Mitsubishi for ductless systems. Your technician will walk you through options that fit your home, budget, and energy goals during the free estimate.
